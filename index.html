Privacy-Preserving Data Sharing in Practice (PDaSP)

Advancing privacy-enhancing technologies and their use to solve real-world problems. Aligned with a recent executive order on AI, the PDaSP program supports practical approaches for privately sharing and analyzing data across a range of use cases and application domains, emphasizing deployment, collaboration, and impact.

Program overview

PDaSP supports practical approaches for privately sharing and analyzing data across a range of use cases. The program emphasizes real-world deployment, collaboration with industry and government partners, and measurable impact in domains such as healthcare, transportation, agriculture, and cybersecurity.

NSF announcement

NSF invests over $10M to advance U.S. leadership in privacy-enhancing technologies

The National Science Foundation announced a $10.4M investment to support inaugural PDaSP awards. Funding supports 10 multidisciplinary teams building practical privacy-preserving data sharing and analytics solutions, emphasizing real-world deployment, collaboration, and community test beds.

Projects by track

PDaSP projects span three tracks focused on foundational technologies, integrated solutions, and testbeds for trustworthy data sharing.

Track 1

Advancing key technologies to enable practical PPDSA solutions

Foundational capabilities that power trustworthy privacy-preserving systems.

Enabling a Privacy-Preserving Data Life Cycle with Lightweight Secure Computation Henry Corrigan-Gibbs and Emma Dauterman, MIT Practical Secure Multiparty Computations for Graph-based Intrusion Detection Systems Yupeng Zhang and Li Zhou, UIUC & UC Irvine
Track 2

Integrated and comprehensive solutions for trustworthy data sharing in application settings

End-to-end systems that enable collaboration across organizations.

Holistic Privacy Preserving Collaborative Data Sharing for Intelligent Transportation Xuegang Ban, Angela Kitali, Yuan Hong, Song Han and collaborators Confidential Genome Imputation and Analytics (CoGIA) Hyunghoon Cho, Suleyman Sahinalp, Fan Zhang, Yale Explainable Auditing of ML Models for Privacy Violations Bradley Malin, Netanel Raviv, Yevgeniy Vorobeychik and partners TIDES - Trusted Integration Data Exchange System Sebastian Angel, Andreas Haeberlen, Brett Falk and team
Track 3

Usable tools and testbeds for trustworthy sharing of private or confidential data

Operational testbeds and tools that make privacy-preserving practice real.

Privacy-Preserving Dairy-Digitalization with Federated Learning Miel Hostens and Joao Dorea, Cornell Testbed for Enhancing Privacy and Robustness of Federated Learning Systems Fatima Anwar, Muhammad Ali Gulzar, Ali Anwar and partners Rigorous and Performant Differentially Private Machine Learning via OpenDP Salil Vadhan and Flavio Calmon, Harvard TEPPIT: Testbed for Privacy-PreservIng Technologies for Data Sharing and Analysis Jelena Mirkovic, John Heidemann, Jose-Luis Ambite, USC
